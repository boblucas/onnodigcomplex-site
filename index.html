Bob Lucassen

I play piano, make word puzzles, and program.

I make things out of language, music, math and code. Puzzles, poems, programs, canons, word lists, proofs. Most of it never left my computer.

It was scattered over MuseScore, an old blog, GitHub, Reddit and my mail. This site collects it in one place that I own. No timeline, just links between things. Dates don't matter much here.

Most of the work is compact and I don't like writing long explanations. If you have questions, ask the assistant. Or me.

About

I'm from Arnhem. Self-taught, no degree. I did the first year of computer science at Radboud at sixteen, without a high school diploma, and wasn't allowed to continue without one. I went to a democratic school. Later I taught programming, games and music at schools like that.

I started at sixteen at MediaMonks. Then Nintendo DS games in C++ at Engine Software, AR at Lukkien, and five years at Esdec building the browser CAD tools that solar installers still use daily. Since 2021 I'm at Alliander, the grid operator, where I lead a small AI research team. The first grid foundation model I trained at home on my own 4090. Now it forecasts the medium-voltage grid in production, on rather more GPUs. Open source as s4casting, paper at CIRED 2025.

In 2020 I built Gigacorpus, a 234 GB Dutch text corpus, still the largest open one. It helped turn Mistral 7B into GEITje, the open Dutch language model. BREIN's lawyers took down the corpus, and later the model. Good story, ask me sometime.

The rest of this site is the useless half, which is my favorite half: canons from constraint solvers, the largest known word square, the only Dutch reflexicon, byte-level models that write palindromes or invent Dutch words, poems in one vowel, an algorithm for generating unlabelled necklaces that two papers had left open. 96/100 on the AIVD kerstpuzzel. I post this kind of thing as onnodigcomplex. Unnecessarily complicated. That's about right.

I'm figuring out what's next. If you have something interesting: mail me.
