Double 4 voice 7/5 phasing modulation canon & how to construct it

https://musescore.com/boblucassen/double-phasing-modulation-canon

This is a very minimal example of a double modulation canon of different modulations and of different lengths that is infinitely repeatable. Specifically the ratio between between the canons is non trivial 5/7.

Every measure of Canon I (5) must be compatible with every measure of Canon II (7). We achieve this by simply picking a single chord progression for each measure, in this case as simple as possible, down a 5th. Then we write all possible voice leading strands (possible first species voices trough given chord progression). We take all these strands and divide them between the canons such that no strand in Canon I can create parallel perfects with a strand in canon II. Using only those movements to create a 2 voice canon gives us the final result.

After this you have essentially a 1st species solution, you can then expand your voice leading strands melodically to create an actual composition.

This method works quite well for more complicated ratios also. And you can of course pick any time unit and more complicated progressions, not just a single measure.

The dual modulation double canon structure works because (+1 semitone * 5 measures) + (-1 semitone * 7 measures) = 12 semitones, which is exactly an octave. But the details regarding that are a whole other thing. For now, modulation canon 1 * duration + modulation canon 2 * duration = unison or octave. And you're good!
