Turns out you can make double modulating canons with different modulations

https://musescore.com/user/6168221/scores/5394255

Surprisingly there is a canonic structure that does not generate conflicting keys when modulating in different directions. The trick is that the canons can be of different lengths and the ratio of those lengths can be inverse to the ratio of their modulations to fit together. Specifically with 3/2 duration ratios you get a period of 6 measures (3 times 2 measures play over 2 times 3 measures). Combine this with 3 semitones up combined with 2 semitones down modulation you get -3 * 2 + 2*3 total semitones moved relative to each other, which is 0 net movement and so it repeats perfectly after those 6 bars and the intermediate keys are related by dominant relationships (because one instance of 3 semitone modulation + 2 instances of 2 semitone modulation = 7 = perfect fifth)). The example is double length, so 6/4 and repeating after 12 bars.

The keys traveled by the upper canon and lower canon will look like this over time for clarity, functionally repeating with both getting back to F.

B A G F B D F
